Bio
cv

During my undergraduate studies, I decided to meet my social service hours requirement in an enclave in the mountains of the Mexican northern state of Chihuahua. There, I was involved in a project that provided solar cell energy to elementary schools. I stayed there for nine weeks, enough time to realize how blessed I am and how to commit myself with those developmental issues that affect our society in general. Social exclusion prevents people from having acceptable living standards that fulfill human potential.

Non-inclusiveness and non-recognition of others' values lead to poverty, violence, crime and corruption. I strive to value the differences of culture and collective thinking by groups with diverse backgrounds. I just feel lucky to have the opportunity and the responsibility to contribute to diversity and inclusion in an academic environment.

In my roles as a professor and researcher, I stress the idea of inclusiveness as a factor for human, social and economic development. I aim to produce a participatory environment in the classroom or in the research teams by opening lines of communication and cooperation. I would like to be proactive in advancing diversity and inclusion in any institution I will work for.

As hispanic, I see the need of participation and collaboration of minority groups in academia to build strong relationships in a more inclusive society.
