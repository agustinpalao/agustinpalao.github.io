Infrastructure, Security & Development

Issues related to Security, Education, Health, Environment, Disaster/Crisis Management have at some point primary root: Development. Peace and prosperity are the focal points of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development . Population in the developing world suffer from deprivations of the most basic needs, and connections to insurgencies or internal conflicts are non-suprising patterns that threaten development and well-being.

I focus on the interdepencies between Goal 9 and Goal 16 of the SDGs. How attacks on civil society and conflict escalation that affect critical infrastructure such as power grids, roads, or pipelines are holding back development progress. At the same time, governments have to work on different strategies to prevent basic services from failure. To learn from conflicts that often target national infrastructure, I study the long-lasting Colombian conflict between government, paramilitary groups and left-wing guerrillas. Colombia has suffered decades of this kind of internal conflict that has been labeled as domestic terrorism.

My research and projects

I use generalized regression models to identify factors that may explain the subversive activity of the different beligerent groups. Modeling human rights violations, threats, pipeline and tower attacks require a non-normal setup. Socio-economic factors at the municipal and department level are captured in multilevel models considering time-variant and time-invariant control variables. The main objectives of this research are the following:

  • Understand the motivations of subversive groups at the local level, compared to a broad and general motivation at a national level
  • Analyze the modus operandi of illegal groups that attack critical infrastructure to inform potential risks to the government and service providers.
  • Find connections with developmental issues that general population suffer to understand the processes of peace agreements and reconciliations after decades of opression and insecurity.
